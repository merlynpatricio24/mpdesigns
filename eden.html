About The Project

The project focuses on redesigning the landing page of Eden Nature Park. The park is located in Davao City, Philippines. With its beautiful forests and wonderful activities in store, it is important that it also translates on to the website of Eden. Given the current state of the website, there are issues concerning both UX and UI. The project focused on providing a new, refreshed, more effective design for web, tablet, and mobile.

Insights On Current Website

-Too much options for navbar.
-Can be shortened.
-Lack of clarity what “STAYCATION”, “When Nature Meets Adventure”, and “When Business and Leisure Meet”.
-Due to generic nature, will be ignored
-Best to put text on the photos in slideshow to make sense of it

-Description of the park is not as engaging and is just in plain text.
-Best to provide more pictures and get people excited on what they can do
-Issues with spacing
-Navbar on scroll makes text more difficult to read.
-Lack of contrast

-Footer looks cluttered due to lack of strong visual hierarchy
-Maps is too small to be relevant.
-Can be moved to a different part of the page
-At the very bottom, links still point to the template origin

Theme

I was aiming for an overall theme for the website that would evoke a specific kind of feeling for the user. I’m aiming for the users to feel a kind of “cozy cabin in the woods” feeling. Its the feeling of warmth and wonder with our surroundings being able to be one with nature. By this specific feeling, users can then have a stronger connection to the park by evoking feelings of warmth, belonging, and wonder.

Logo Change

To add consistency on the website as well as giving the logo a more refreshed and modern approach, I made the logo more simple with a touch of that old vintage feel. I took out the description of Eden as a “Nature Park & Resort” primarily because given the current situation with Eden being one of the 2 biggest mountain resorts in Davao, people have a good idea of what Eden offers. I then focused more attention to the name as well as its signature mountain image with both elements seamlessly cooperating with one another to create an amazing logo.
