The Product

Value Proposition

Take Eat Easy is an app that changes the way we do grocery shopping by making life faster and easier for the average shopper. It's an all-in-one companion in grocery shopping complete with all the important things you need when doing your groceries. The app makes things faster by providing a scanning system for both products and transactions as well as intuitive tools that you can customize to your needs.

Scan Feature

With the app, you scan your phone to get inside the store as well as make the final transaction using the store scanner by the exit. You scan the barcode of each product you want to buy and when you check out, you will be using your phone to make the transaction.

Recipe Feature

Browse through various recipes categorized into different specialities. You can save recipes as well as integrate the ingredients into your grocery list.

Checklist Feature

Keep check of everything you need with our list feature. You can specify the quantity of the product as well as save lists that repeat items.

Discounts Feature

Stay up to date with all the discounted products that are available in the store. Products automatically update in terms of expiry time for the offer. These things can then easily be added to the checklist for when you shop.

Product Locator Feature

With some products being difficult to find, the app makes it easier for you to find what you're looking for by providing a map of the store and where the product is. It also shows which products in their directory has discounts.

Grocery Bag

When looking at your grocery bag which is the "cart" equivalent in e-commerce, it will show you your current scanned products, your budget status (once it is set), and products that you haven't added yet based on your checklist. When you're good to go, you then scan your phone to the checkout counter and off you go.

About The Project

Grocery shopping is a must for every person and with that has its own problems. We have grown accustomed to the little frustrations we would get when doing groceries. In this project, I've asked people what they feel about their grocery shopping experience, figure out what are their pains and gains, and provide solutions to these problems.

The Objective

Provide a UX design solution for the time- consuming payment and checkout process in the store. The solution should deliver a new digital check-out experience with mobile payment innovations which users can interact with in store.

Methods Used

User Interviews

Conducted 5 in depth interviews with various users within the target market.

Wireframing

Creating both designs in high fidelity and in low fidelity to be able to come up with solutions to the problems of users to have a great experience.

User Personas

User archetypes that have details on the kind of profile that specific type of customer has for deeper understanding of the target market.

User Journey Maps

A flow showing the journey of the user from point A to point B highlighting possible pains and gains with varying degrees.

Word Clouds

Using the data that I've gathered from my research, I pin down specific words that pop up most of the time in varying themes to see how customers perceive the experience.

Web Scraping

Looking into forums and blog posts that show people's thoughts about grocery shopping and aggregating all of that data into one.

Overall Key Findings

Based on the data collected

Pains

  • Very long queue
  • Cannot fid product
  • Products are out of stock
  • Getting lost in the store
  • Lack of staff support
  • Poor labeling
  • Product placing

Gains

  • Product deals
  • New products
  • Fresh products
  • Positive atmosphere

Getting to the Final Output

How I led to these designs

Low Fidelity Wireframing

Created wireframes that were easy to alter. Talked with the client present and figured out how to solve the problems found from the research. This process had consistent iterations.

High Fidelity Wireframing

After several cycles of iterations, the batch of designs were made into its high fidelity state and was further tested.
